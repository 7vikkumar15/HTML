Facts about tigers

They are the world's largest wild cats, each individual has a unique stripe pattern like a fingerprint, they are excellent swimmers and enjoy water unlike most other cats, tigers are solitary creatures who prefer to live and hunt alone, and their powerful roars can be heard for miles, sometimes temporarily paralyzing their prey

Tigers are the largest cats, known for their unique stripes and strength, and are the only big cats that love water and swim well. They are solitary, territorial carnivores that hunt large prey and use their exceptional sight, hearing, and powerful jaws to kill. While they were once numerous, tigers are now critically endangered, with most living in India.
